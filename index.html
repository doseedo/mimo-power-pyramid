A Structural Analysis

The Global Power Pyramid

Who controls what — and how power concentrates toward the apex

🏦 I Financial Capital
BIS · Central Banks · BlackRock · Vanguard · State Street · IMF · World Bank
Controls reserve currencies, interest rates, sovereign debt terms, and the rules of the global monetary system. Asset managers hold decisive ownership stakes in virtually every major corporation on Earth.
🌍 II Nation-States
United States · United Kingdom · Israel · Saudi Arabia · China · European Union
Military projection · Currency sovereignty · Sanctions enforcement
Nominally sovereign governments that hold the monopoly on legitimate force, control border regimes, and backstop reserve currencies. In practice, constrained upward by bond markets and downward by electoral cycles.
👁 III Intelligence & Influence
CIA · Mossad · MI6 · NSA · FSB | CFR · Brookings · RAND
6 Media Corporations (~90% of U.S. media) · Platform algorithms
Shapes what is knowable, sayable, and thinkable at scale. Intelligence agencies influence elections, destabilize adversaries, and run covert economic warfare. Think tanks launder policy into consensus. Media sets the Overton window.
🏛 IV Political Layer
Congress · Presidents · Prime Ministers · Courts · Regulatory Agencies
Downstream of the layer above — translating approved policy into law
The visible face of governance. Elected officials operate within windows of acceptable action defined by campaign finance, media narratives, and intelligence briefings. Courts and regulators provide institutional legitimacy.
⚙ V Corporate Layer
Lockheed Martin · Raytheon · Boeing · Palantir | Pfizer · Google · Meta · Microsoft
Shell · Saudi Aramco · JPMorgan · Goldman Sachs
Executes the industrial and digital infrastructure of power. Defense contractors convert tax revenue into weapons systems. Tech platforms mine behavioral data. Pharma monetizes biological risk. Energy majors underwrite geopolitical alignment.
⚔ VI Proxy Armies & NGOs
NATO · IDF · Gulf Armies · Wagner Group · Funded Militias | NED-funded NGOs · Color Revolution infrastructure
Proxy conflicts · IMF debt traps · Regime-change pipelines
The operational arm of geopolitical strategy. Military alliances project force without direct accountability. NGOs provide civil-society cover for destabilization. Debt instruments enforce compliance without occupation.
👥 VII The Public
You  ·  Taxpayers  ·  Consumers  ·  Soldiers  ·  Voters  ·  Patients  ·  Users
Fund it all  ·  Fight the wars  ·  Buy the products  ·  Provide the data  ·  Carry the debt
The broadest base and the ultimate resource. Labor, attention, tax revenue, military service, consumer spending, and biological data flow upward. In return: managed narratives, legal protections calibrated to compliance, and the periodical right to choose between pre-approved candidates.
Money flows UP  ·  Consequences flow DOWN
Hover each tier to expand  ·  The pyramid is structural, not conspiratorial
